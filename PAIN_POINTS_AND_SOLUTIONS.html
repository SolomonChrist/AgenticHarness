Agentic Harness

From fragile AI setups to a system you can actually recover, inspect, and keep running.

A simple outline of the pain points users face with traditional harness systems and the practical answers Agentic Harness gives through a file-first, local-first control plane.

Core Pain Points

Vendor lock

The work feels trapped inside one vendor’s runtime, pricing, and token model.

Agentic Harness keeps the system in local files so another harness can take over later.

Fear of losing work

Users don’t know what to back up, and critical context lives in hidden session state.

Tasks, memory, and coordination live in markdown, so backups are obvious and portable.

Hard restarts

When bots die or sessions end, it’s unclear how to recover or continue.

Leases, event logs, and memory give new harnesses enough state to resume the work.

Hidden complexity

The system works, but the user no longer understands its moving parts.

Agentic Harness names the layers clearly: Core, Projects, Runner, Telegram, Visualizer.

Scaling overwhelm

As more work arrives, the user doesn’t know which bots to make or how to route it.

Chief_of_Staff recommends roles, tracks work, and grows the harness catalog over time.

No visibility

The swarm may be “working,” but there is no clear picture of what is happening.

Telegram provides remote communication, and the Visualizer adds 2D, 3D, and VR visibility on top of the same files.

Agentic Harness is the backup-friendly, vendor-flexible control plane for AI work that has to survive real life.
